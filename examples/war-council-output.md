# War Council Output — Example

> This is what you get when you say "call war-council — should I launch a free tier?"

---

## Question: Should I launch a free tier for LaunchKit?

### The Operator

Launch it. Here's why:
- Current conversion: 2% of landing page visitors sign up. A free tier removes the biggest objection (price).
- Implementation: 3 days max. Limit to 1 project, no team features, no API access.
- Risk is low — free users cost almost nothing on Supabase free tier. If it doesn't convert, kill it in 30 days.

**Recommendation:** Ship a free tier this week. Cap it hard.

### The Strategist

Wait. Here's why:
- Free tiers attract the wrong users. They want "free forever", not "free to try." You'll spend support time on people who'll never pay.
- At $3.2K MRR, you don't have a conversion problem — you have a traffic problem. 2% of 500 visitors = 10 signups. 2% of 5,000 visitors = 100 signups. Fix traffic first.
- Better play: 14-day trial of the full product. Creates urgency. Free tier creates complacency.
- Long-term: a free tier is easy to launch and hard to kill. Users feel entitled to it.

**Recommendation:** 14-day free trial instead. Invest the time in content/SEO to drive traffic.

### The Critic

Both options assume the product is ready for more users. Is it?
- Onboarding just got fixed this week. Has it been tested with real users?
- Support capacity: who handles free user questions? You're solo.
- The real risk isn't "free tier vs trial" — it's scaling users before the product is solid. One bad review on Product Hunt and you're done.
- Key assumption to test: do people actually want this enough to sign up? Run a waitlist first. 100 signups = green light.

**Recommendation:** Waitlist for 2 weeks. If 100+ sign up, launch the trial. If <100, the problem isn't pricing — it's positioning.

---

## VERDICT: 14-day free trial (not free tier)

**CONFIDENCE:** Medium — the Critic's point about readiness is valid.

**KEY RISK:** If onboarding is still buggy, trial users churn and you lose the "first impression" window.

**NEXT ACTION:** Run the onboarding flow yourself as a new user. Fix any friction. Then launch the trial next Monday.
